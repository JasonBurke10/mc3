3.    Using the combined group of suspected bad actors you created in question 2, show the interactions within the group over time.

a. Characterize the groups organizational structure and show a full picture of communications within the group.

b. Does the group composition change during the course of their activities?

c. How do the groups interactions change over time?

 

The above image depicts suspicious communications surrounding the suspicious purchase that took place on September 20th (depicted by a red square on the chart). We chose to analyze Rosalias communications during this time as she was involved in the suspicious purchase. This image depicts her sent communications (her received will be analyzed below). It can be seen that on the days leading up to the purchase, Rosalia was involved in multiple communications, and then the day of the purchase she sent 3 communications. Following this event, she continued to make communications every day that followed. The significance of this is that Rosalias outward communications spiked significantly on the day of the purchase. This shows a potential trend that people involved in suspicious activity tend to have a boost in outbound communications on the day of a suspicious purchase.

 

The above image depicts Rosalias received communications on the days surrounding the same suspicious purchase. This chart shows a slightly different potential trend. Although the same pattern is seen that her communications spiked the day of the purchase, it can also be seen that a few days prior to the purchase she had a drastic spike in her inbound communications. These two charts together imply that there may be a trend of a spike in received communications prior to a suspicious purchase, followed by a spike in both sent and received on the day of the purchase. After the day of the purchase, her communications level off again, with some days having none at all.

 

This image depicts the suspicious communications of all suspicious people in the days surrounding the suspicious purchase on September 20th. It can be seen that several days prior to this purchase there was a large jump from one to four communications. A few days later, when the purchase occurs, there is an even larger jump to five communications. Being there is only one suspicious purchase to analyze, we chose to use the same analysis for a suspicious meeting to see if this is a trend among all suspicious activities (seen below).

 

 

The image above analyzes the days surrounding a suspicious meeting occurring on November 12th, 2015 (indicated by a red diamond). The objective of this chart is to determine a trend in suspicious communications of the entire group around a suspicious meeting. This chart shows a similar trend to the analysis of the days surrounding the purchase, showing a large spike in communications prior to the meeting that then levels out back to normal afterward. A spike in communications in days leading up to, as well as the day of, a meeting or purchase seems to be a trend among suspicious activities.
